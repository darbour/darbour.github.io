About Me

I am currently a researcher at Adobe research where my work focuses on the intersection between causal inference/discovery and machine learning with a particular focus on dependent data. Previously I was a research scientist in the Core Data Science group at Facebook. My work at Facebook focused on developing methods for advanced experimentation, specifically Bayesian optimization and contextual bandits. I earned a PhD from the College of Information and Computer Sciences of UMass Amherst, where I was advised by David Jensen. My research in grad school focused on methods for causal inference from observational relational data. Specifically: (1) hypothesis testing for dependence and causal direction in relational domains, (2) learning the structure of relational causal models, and (3) effect estimation in the presence of confounding from peer influences.
